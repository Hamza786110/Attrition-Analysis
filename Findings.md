# Attrition Analysis | SQL-Based HR Analytics Project

**Objective**  
Conducted comprehensive SQL analysis on employee dataset to identify attrition patterns, quantify key risk factors, and deliver actionable recommendations to reduce employee turnover.

## Tools & Technologies
- SQL (MySQL / PostgreSQL)
- Excel
- Power BI (Interactive Dashboard)

## Project Structure
- **Data/**: Raw employee dataset (CSV)
- **Queries/**: All SQL queries used for analysis
- **Results/**: Output tables and insights
- **Dashboard/**: Power BI visualizations for HR stakeholders

## Key Findings

- Overall attrition rate stood at **16.1%**, with a total of **237 employees** leaving the organization.
- Sales and Research & Development departments recorded the highest attrition rates among all units.
- Sales Representatives and Laboratory Technicians showed the highest job-role attrition, exceeding other positions significantly.
- Employees with **0–2 years** of tenure had the highest turnover probability, especially in early career stages.
- Employees working overtime faced a much higher attrition rate of **31%**, compared to only **10%** for non-overtime employees.

## Recommendations

1. Launch targeted retention programs in **Sales** and **Research & Development** departments to address their high attrition levels.
2. Strengthen onboarding and mentorship initiatives for employees in the first **0–3 years** to reduce early-stage turnover.
3. Review and optimize overtime policies, aiming to lower the **31%** attrition risk associated with excessive overtime.
4. Introduce department-specific engagement and job satisfaction improvement programs, with special focus on Sales.
5. Implement the Power BI dashboard for continuous monitoring of attrition metrics and early identification of at-risk employees.

## Impact Potential
These data-driven actions can significantly bring down the current **16.1%** attrition rate, lower hiring costs, and improve overall workforce stability.

## Project Outcome
Successfully transformed raw HR data into clear, quantifiable insights using SQL queries, enabling better decision-making for employee retention.
